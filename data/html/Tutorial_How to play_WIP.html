Introduction

Satisfactory is a factory-building game, centered around automation, exploration and expansion. Build a factory to automate the production of various resources required to progress through Milestones , culminating in a fully-upgraded Space Elevator for Project Assembly. As each Milestone tier is completed, more contents are unlocked. The requirements to unlock the next tier become increasingly complex and time-consuming to fulfill, thus it is advised to expand the factory to produce the required parts automatically and efficiently.

The player-controlled game character, the Pioneer , lands on an alien planet named MASSAGE-2(A-B)b with only basic tools to build The HUB . The game narrator, ADA , provides guidance throughout Tier 0 . Tier 1 to Tier 8 are left for the Pioneer to explore. Game content is mostly unlocked via Milestones and Researches . Higher tiers of Milestones, in turn, are unlocked at the Space Elevator. Additional contents can be unlocked at the AWESOME Shop .

There are collectibles scattered around the World which can be collected to increase factory efficiency, such as Power Slug s and Hard Drive s. There are hostile creatures guarding these resources, which makes combat and exploration part of the gameplay.

  • Satisfactory is an open-ended game. As there is no actual 'Win' condition, players are not required to strictly follow this guide.
  • The writer of this article is left handed and is using custom keys. However, default control keys are referred in this guide.
  • The writer uses flying cheat to take the screenshots. Pioneer will be able to reach a higher elevation using Ladder or Jetpack .
  • Spoilers warning : If you wish to explore the game mechanics by yourself, stop reading now.
  • Disclaimer: This guide is written by community members and does not represent the 'Official Guide' in any way.

Game Settings

  • At the main menu, review the Options -> controls , video, and audio settings. Adjust where necessary.

Recommended settings:

  • Gameplay
    • Head Bobbing: Off
    • Autosave Interval: 2 minutes (can be increased to 5 mins or 10 mins post Hard Drive collection)
    • Build In One Click: Yes
  • Audio
    • Enable Directional Subtitles: Yes
  • Video
    • View Distance: High / Ultra
    • Motion Blur: Off
  • Controls
    • Hold To Sprint: Off
  • Keybindings
    • Copy Factory Clipboard: Change to any single key instead of Ctrl+C
    • Paste Factory Clipboard: Change to any single key instead of Ctrl+V
  • User Interface
    • Use Small Equipment Icons in the HUD: Yes
    • Item Abbreviation: Yes

Starting the game

  • From the main menu, click 'New Game -> 2nd area: Rocky Desert'
    • All 4 starting areas are connected in the same world and you are free to explore to the other areas in the game.
  • Enter session name
  • Choose Session Type:
    • Private: For single-player Pioneer
    • Friends Only: For multiplayer. Officially supports up to 4 Pioneers
  • Skip Intro: Off. This actually skips the Tier 0 . For first time Pioneer, going through Tier 0 is recommended.
  • Click 'Start Game'
  • Wait for the cinematic to end. The Pioneer , the game character, will pick up the Build Gun and exit the Drop-pod , where the game starts.
  • Move the mouse to look around, and find the rock pillar as shown in the below thumbnail. If it cannot be seen, restart the game until this rock pillar can be seen from landing location. It should be located South-West. Refer to the Compass at the top of the screen.
    • While in the Drop-pod, press Esc and click the red 'Skip Intro' will skip the cinematic and Pioneer will land on ground. Tier 0 will not be skipped.
  • Once the rock pillar can be seen, while still near the Drop-pod, save the game.
    • Press Esc , Save Game, then enter a name to save.
    • There are 3 autosaves that may be used to re-load in case the Pioneer died or encounter other accidents.
    • Save files are located at %localappdata%\FactoryGame\Saved\SaveGames\<your id>

Onboarding (Tier 0)

  • The game stage before Tier 1 is called Onboarding , where the game narrator ADA will provide hints and guidance.
    • Refer to the left of the screen for hints or instructions.
  • Use W A S D to move. Use mouse to look around.
  • At this stage, most of the control keys are locked. More controls will be unlocked later.
    • Default keybindings will be referred in this guide. Custom-mapped controls will be shown at the bottom right.
  • Look at the Drop-pod and dismantle F it. Target(s) to be dismantled are highlighted in orange. Hold F to dismantle. After the drop-pod is dismantled, exit Esc the dismantle mode.
    • All dismantle actions refund the full cost of the buildable; no material is wasted. While dismantling, observe the materials that are being refunded.
    • If you moved too far from the drop pod, look at the Compass above the screen to locate its direction.
  • Open the Codex O . Explore its function and read through the pages.
  • Close Esc the Codex window.
  • Open Inventory Tab ↹ . Drag the Xeno-Zapper to the 'Hands' slot at the right.
  • Try the following:
    • Double-click the Xeno-Zapper in the hand slot to de-equip it.
    • Click 'Sort' at the bottom left to sort items.
    • Double-click the Xeno-Zapper in the inventory to equip it again.
    • At the right, Head, Body, Back and Legs equipments will be unlocked later, just ignore them now.
    • Near the bottom middle is the trash can. Items dragged to it will be deleted permanently. Do not use it now.
    • Drag the HUB Parts outside the inventory window to drop it onto the ground.
    • Item dropped to the ground will never respawn; there is no risk losing it.
  • Close Esc the inventory.
    • The in-game chat ↵ Enter function is now unlocked.
  • Pick up the HUB Parts E . Make sure it is in the inventory.
    • Items being picked up will be shown at lower left.
  • Holster H the weapon. Press H again to bring it out.
  • Open Photo Mode P , use mousewheel to zoom in / out. Left click to take a screenshot. Then close it P .

  • Learn the game's Head-Up Display :
    • At the lower left is the pioneer's health bar. It has 100 HP, divided into 10 parts. Pioneer dies when HP reaches 0. Right above it is the equipped hand slot, the Xeno-Zapper.
    • At the top left is the current game version . In a multiplayer session, all clients must be running the same version as the host.
    • At the top is the Compass. Look around and identify the E, W, N, S directions.
    • At the bottom right displays the keybindings for static key shortcuts.

  • Press ⇧ Shift to toggle run.
  • Try the following movements:
    • Crouch C . Then, move around while crouching.
    • Jump vertically then crouch mid-air while pressing forward.
    • Slide. Run, then crouch.
    • While running forward, jump Space .
    • Slide-jump. Run forward W , then crouch, then immediately jump. Observe the jump is now further and higher.
    • Bunny-hop, a slide jump followed by another slide jump. This is slightly faster than running. Bunny hop will not work on uphill slopes.
    • Double zap. Spam attack key . Note that after every 2 attacks, there is a slight cooldown.
    • Try zap while running backwards. Try zap while jumping mid-air. Try bunny hop followed by immediate zap. Be familiar with these attack actions.
    • Perform emote . Hold T then choose one of the emote then release T .

  • Move to other nearby woods to continue picking them up.
    • Wood , Leaves , some other plants, and Alien Remains are collectively called Biomass which can be used later for Power productions.
  • Be ready to fight enemy aliens. Keep an eye on the HP bar.

First encounter

  • Before moving towards the rock pillar as shown above , learn the following aliens and their attacks.
  • There are 3 types of hostile creatures at the moment. Hogs , Spitters and Flying Crab Hatchers .
  • Hog : Bumps to Pioneer to deal 10 damage. Move sideway or backwards and attack it. It takes 4 zaps to kill. Avoid dealing more than 2 hogs at once.
  • Spitter : Shoot at Pioneer to deal 3 damage. Just spam melee attacks at it. It takes 6 hits to kill.
  • Crab Hatcher : Spawn 3 Flying Crab s which suicide bombs at Pioneer, dealing 30 damages in total. It then spawn the next wave every 5 seconds. Ignore the crabs, bunny hop to the Hatcher and spam melee attacks. It takes 4 hits to kill. Avoid dealing more than 2 hatchers at once.
  • Other aliens, such as Lizard Doggo , Bean and Non Flying Bird are harmless.
  • After killing the alien, loot the drops, then keep them for later use.
  • Eat Beryl Nuts to heal.
    • Even without eating, there is a high HP auto regen from 1~30 HP, and slow regen from 31~100 HP.
  • If the Pioneer died, reload the latest autosave.
  • Move towards the rock pillar while picking up Woods , Leaves and Beryl Nuts .
    • Follow the path as shown below to reach the rock pillar.
    • Aim for 500-ish leaves, 200-ish woods and 30-ish nuts for a good start.
  • Once reaching the rock pillar, save the game.

Mining Iron Ore and the HUB Upgrade 1

  • Scan V for Iron Ore again. Observe the pinged locations, there are 6 Iron nodes nearby.
  • Check all the 6 Iron nodes. One of the Iron node is not clearly visible and can be ignored at this stage.
  • Approach the nearest Iron deposit, the rocky chunk above the Iron node. Hand mine (hold E ) it and remove deposits above each nodes. Kill the guarding hatchers .
    • After mining all 5 deposits, there should be around 100~130 Iron Ore in inventory.
  • Optional: look down at the Iron node. Mine it (Hold E ) and observe 2 Iron Ores are added to inventory.
    • This is considerably slower than mining the deposit. Stop hand-mining first.
  • Flashlight is now unlocked B . Try turning it on and off.
  • Open build menu Q , click The HUB , then build the HUB beside the rock pillar as shown.
    • Use mousewheel to rotate its direction. The 'balcony' side should be facing out.
    • If the HUB is built wrong, dismantle F it then rebuild.
    • Only one HUB can be built in the world.
  • Stand in front of the HUB's terminal and open it E .
  • Observe the milestone cost and what it unlocks. Click 'Select Milestone'. The greyed-out icon shows it needs 10 Iron Rod s. Close it Esc .
  • Look behind, stand in front of the HUB's Craft Bench and open it E .
    • Explore its function, try clicking different buttons in it.
    • In multiplayer, build more Craft Bench on the ground, as each Craft Bench can only be occupied by a Pioneer. (Build menu -> Production -> Craft Bench)
  • Look at Iron Rod, it is greyed out. Point to the crafting requirement, observe 1 Iron Rod needs 1 Iron Ingot to make.
  • As 10 Iron Rods need 10 Iron Ingots, make 10 Ingots. Click Iron Ingot. Observe 1 Iron Ingot needs 1 Iron Ore to make.
  • Mouse over the yellow 'Hammer' button. To start crafting, hit Space once. It will start crafting Ingots continuously.
    • Observe the inventory items at the right. Ingots are added to the inventory while Ores are being consumed.
    • Optional: Turn all the Ores into Ingots. You may afk for 2 minutes during the crafting process.
  • After 10 Iron Ingots are made, hit Space again to stop.
  • Craft 10 Iron Rods. Observe Iron Rods are made faster as each Rod requires 1 'Hammer', while Ingot requires 3 Hammers.
    • Optional: Craft 50 Iron Rods and craft the remaining ingots into Iron Plates.
  • Open the HUB's terminal. Look at the top right 'Relevant Items', shows the Iron Rods.
  • Mouse over the Iron Rod icon, then Ctrl + it, to transfer them to the terminal.
    • Items transferred to the HUB's terminal cannot be taken back.
  • Click the yellow arrow 'Upgrade HUB'.
  • Open the terminal again, select 'HUB Upgrade 2', then 'Select Milestone'.
    • The current Milestone requirement is displayed at the top right.
    • Do not rush for HUB upgrade 2. Use the materials to automate the mining process. Automation comes before progression.
  • Mine out the deposit to remove them

Portable Miner and the HUB Upgrade 2

  • After the Upgrade, observe the changes on The HUB.
  • A Personal Storage Box is added beside the Terminal. Open the box. Organize the Pioneer 's Inventory and store unwanted items into it.
    • Observe the dynamic shortcut keys displayed below the UI, such as transfer stack, split stack, etc.
    • If the box is full, excess items can be dropped to the ground, they will not disappear.
  • An upgraded HUB will not lose its progress even if it is dismantled then rebuilt elsewhere. Same holds true for Space Elevator .
  • Make use of newly unlocked Portable Miner to semi-automate the mining process:
    • First, build an Equipment Workshop near The HUB. Prepare necessary materials at the Craft Bench .
    • Workout the necessary Iron Ingots required to make them. (13 Iron Ingots)
  • Find the Equipment Workshop in the build menu, under 'Production' tab.
    • Alternatively, use the Search Box N . Type in 'Equipment Workshop', the result usually appears before you type its full name. Then click on it.
    • The Search Box can also be used as an arithmetic calculator; try it out.
  • If the Workshop's hologram intersects with the HUB, it will turn red, means it cannot be built there. Move the hologram away until it turns blue, then click to build.
    • Recall any buildings can be dismantled F for a full-refund.
  • Open the Equipment Workshop and craft 1 Portable Miner.
    • Equipment Workshop and Craft Bench do not share functions; means Iron Rods and Plates cannot be crafted here. Go back to Craft Bench and craft some Iron Plates and Iron Rods.
  • Equip the Portable Miner into hand slot.
    • Be careful as this will disarm the Xeno-Zapper. When facing aliens, quickly equip back the Xeno-Zapper.
  • Look at the nearest Iron node, place the Portable Miner down . Preferably close to the edge.
    • When it shows 'Press and Hold E to pick up Iron Ore', that is a valid place to put down a miner.
  • Open the Portable Miner E and click 'Grab All' to take out Iron Ores.
    • Portable Miner will mine up to 1 stack of Iron Ore (100), then pause mining. Once the Ores are removed, it will continue mining.
    • The 'Pick Up Miner' will pick up both the Portable Miner and its contained Ores.
  • Craft more Iron Rods and Plates and complete the HUB Upgrade 2.
  • Optional: When looting biomass near around the base, intentionally left out one plant nearby each rock. This allows rocks and plants to be Chainsawed out together later.
  • N to Quick Search. Type building name, then click to build
  • Build an Equipment Workshop beside The HUB
  • Equipment Workshop UI. Craft a Portable Miner
  • Place Portable Miner on Iron node
  • Portable Miner UI. Click 'Grab All' to take out Iron Ores

Smelter and the HUB Upgrade 3

  • Making Iron Ingots manually is time-consuming. We will make use of Smelter to automate this process.
  • Smelter requires Wire which requires Copper Ore to make. Scan V for Copper Ore.
    • Hold V and a selection wheel will pop out. Move the mouse towards 'Copper Ore' then release the V key.
    • Use the Compass to locate the search results. Save the game.
  • Find the two Copper nodes nearby and remove their deposits.
  • While moving towards the Copper node, some creepy voice can be heard. Just ignore these voices.
    • This is related to some unfinished content, Mercer Sphere and Somersloop . ADA is urging the Pioneer to pick these up.
    • The one producing the voice is located above one of the rock pillar. Picking this up will silence the voices.
  • While walking around the base, pick up Leaves and Woods. You will need a lot of them.
  • Craft some Wires to build a Smelter. Craft more Wires and craft them into Cables.
  • When building a Smelter, observe it has a input port (orange) and output port (green). These are for conveyor belts and can be ignored at this stage.
  • Build a Power Line, which connects the HUB's Biomass Burner and the Smelter. Just build one Smelter now, as more can be built later.
  • Open the Smelter and select 'Iron Ingot' recipe.
    • Alternatively, try type 'Iron Ingot' in the search bar to filter the desired recipe.
    • If clicked the wrong recipe, click the 'Select Recipe' tab at the top to re-select.
    • Observe the crafting speed and Power consumption.
    • The 'Standby' switch at the bottom right can be used to turn on/off this machine.
  • Ctrl + some Iron Ores into the Smelter.
  • It displays 'No Power'. To generate power, bring some Leaves and feed into the Biomass Burner .
    • Avoid feeding Woods or other items into it, as they are useful for later stage.
    • Observe the burning rate and power generated.
    • Each idle machine consumes 0.1 MW.
  • Open the Smelter and take out the Iron Ingots. Refill the Iron Ores into the Smelter.
    • When the Smelter contains a full stack of Ingots (100), it will pause crafting. Remove Ingots to let it continue.
    • All other machines / factory buildings does the same. They stop crafting when their output contain a full stack.
  • Craft 6 or more Portable Miners.
    • Place 2 additional Portable Miners on Iron node, and 2 Portable Miners at Copper node.
  • South-west from The HUB contains 2 Limestone nodes. Mine out their deposits. Put 2 Portable Miners on the closest Limestone node.
  • Complete HUB Upgrade 3.
  • Hold V then mouse select 'Copper' to scan for it
  • Picking up the Somersloop will silence ADA 's voice, but need to unlock Ladder first
  • Build Smelter and connect it to Biomass Burner with Power Line
  • Smelter UI. Select recipe - Iron Ingot
  • Feed Iron Ores to Smelter
  • Take out Iron Ingot from Smelter
  • Biomass Burner UI. Feed Leaves into Biomass Burner
  • Remove Limestone deposits. Place Portable Miners on Limestone node

Power Pole and HUB Upgrade 4

  • Power Pole is now unlocked, which allows branching of electricity. Now more buildings (Smelters) can be connected to the Burner.
  • Prepare to build more Smelters to quadruple the Ingot production rate.
    • Try use To-Do List . In the build menu, press the '+' beside Smelter 3 times, and beside the Constructor once.
    • Navigate Build menu -> Power. ⇧ Shift + the '+' beside Power Pole Mk.1. This adds 10 of it.
    • Add 20 Power Lines by ⇧ Shift + two times.
  • To-Do List appears at the right. This tells how much material is needed to build all of these. Craft them at the Craft Bench .
  • While doing so, remember to keep the built Smelter busy by feeding it Ores and take out the Ingots.
  • The first night may have arrived by now. Recall the flashlight can be toggled with B .
  • Start building more Smelter. Try the Copy / Eyedropper function: Aim at the existing Smelter and click middle mouse button .
  • Hold the 'Snap To Guidelines' dynamic key Ctrl then aim at the side of Smelter. Click 3 times to build 3 of them in a row, aligned to the first.
    • Even when the snapping mode is active, the building hologram can be rotated with mousewheel .
  • Set the last Smelter to 'Copper Ingot' and the remaining as 'Iron Ingot'.
  • Recall the shortcut key 'Copy Factory Clipboard' (default: Ctrl + C ) you have set before , aim at a Iron Ingot Smelter and copy its setting.
  • Paste the settings (default: Ctrl + V ) to the remaining Smelters. There is no need to open the Smelters UI, just aim at Smelters and copy-paste.
  • Now rebuild the Power network. Dismantle F the existing Power Line between the Burner and Smelter.
    • As factory buildings cannot be Daisy Chained in game, they must be connected to a nearby Power Pole.
    • A Power Pole can be connected to another Power Pole.
    • The max length for a single Power Line is 100 meters. Build Power Poles to extend its reach.
  • Refer to the below screenshot for connection method.
  • A Power Pole Mk.1 has 4 connections. Reserve 2 of it for Poles' daisy chaining. Connect at most 2 buildings to a Power Pole, or less if future branching is planned.
  • Feed the Smelters and craft more Ingots.
  • Build a Constructor in front of Limestone node and set it to Concrete . Feed the Limestones from Portable Miners into it.
    • Concrete cannot be crafted at Smelter. Observe different machines are used to craft different items.
  • Remember to feed the Biomass Burner regularly with Leaves .
  • Check out the Power Graph from Biomass Burner's UI to make sure the Power Consumption is less than Capacity. Avoid overbuild at this stage.
    • The same graph can be seen by opening up the Power Pole's UI.
  • Try the 'probing' function. Enter Dismantle mode F and aim at a building, but do not dismantle it. It will shows the input and output slot's contents of the building.
    • Try probing a Smelter to check how much Ore / Ingot it contains. Try probing a Biomass Burner to check how much Leaves are remaining.
  • Complete the HUB Upgrade 4.
  • Click on the '+' beside buildings to add them to To-Do List
  • A list of material required to build the listed buildings
  • While building, hold Ctrl to snap to guidelines
  • Dismantle the Power Line
  • Connect Smelters and Burner with Power Poles and Power Lines. Plan for future connections.
  • Build a Constructor near the Limestone node and connect it with power
  • Concrete is crafted at Constructor
  • Use dismantle mode F to probe the contents of a building. Do not dismantle it.

Power trip and breaker reset

The sound played when a power trip occurs.
  • Power trip may happen from time to time. 'Fuse Broken' warning will be displayed at the left and a 'Shut Down' sound is played.
  • To restore power, check the following:
  1. Make sure all power generators (such as Biomass Burner) are properly feed / fueled. Probe them F to check the remaining fuels.
  2. Make sure not to overbuild. Read the power graph. Ensure power consumption is less than power production. Disconnect Power Lines where necessary.
  3. Else, increase power generation capacity by building more generators (such as Burner). This tech will be unlocked later.
  • Use the mouse, click and pull the lever down to restore power.
    • If the power trips again, check the steps 1-3 above.
  • Do not construct additional buildings due to power limitation. Proceed to complete the HUB Upgrade 5.
    • At this moment, the power capacity is 20 MW (from HUB's Biomass Burner) and each Smelter / Constructor takes 4 MW each. Divide 20 by 4 shows it can supports up to 5 machines.
  • Check the fuels and total power consumption, then pull down the lever to restore power

Building hotbar and shortcuts

  • Try configure the building hotbar. The hotbar is located at the bottom.
  • Open build menu, mouse over Smelter , and press Num 5 (or whichever number you preferred). Observe the Smelter icon is appeared at slot 5.
  • To build Smelter using hotkey, exit build menu. Press Num 5 to start building.
  • Now try to set Constructor to Num 5 . The hotkey is now replaced.
  • Set Constructors and Smelters to the hotkeys you preferred.
  • Notice the hotkey 1-4 has already been set. They can be re-configured as well.
  • If the hotbar full, use Alt + to scroll to different hotbar. There are 10 hotbars in total.
  • Try the quick switch function:
    • While building a Power Line , hold E to bring out the 'Quick Switch' wheel.
    • Move the mouse and select the desired buildings.
    • Buildings under the same sub-category in the build menu, will appear in the same wheel.
  • Recall the 5 different methods to start a building construction:
    • Build menu Q
    • Quick search N and type in the building's name
    • Eyedropper click
    • Building hotkeys Num 1 to Num 0
    • Quick switch from other buildings (hold E )
  • Use Quick Switch (hold E ) to switch to other buildings in the same sub-category

Conveyor Belts and HUB Upgrade 6

  • Now the second (and the last) Biomass Burner is added to the HUB, five more machines can be built.
  • To add the 2nd Biomass Burner to the Power network (also called Power grid), build a Power Pole at the middle of an existing Power Line as shown. Connect the 2nd Biomass Burner to the closest Pole with a Power Line.
  • Feed the 2nd Biomass Burner with leaves. Read the power graph and observe the power capacity is now 40 MW.
  • Prepare to build 5 additional Constructors to automate the production of Iron Rods, Iron Plates and Wires.

    • 5 Constructors, 10 Power Poles, 20 Power Lines, 50 Conveyor Belts

    • Hold F to dismantle the highlighted buildings.
    • Dismantle the Power Poles near the Smelters, except the one connected to the Burner.

    • Crates cannot be dismantled; they will only disappear when the item within are looted.

    • If directions is incorrect, it will cause connection issue when building the belt.
    • Sometimes the belt cannot be built due to weird shape / too steep. Relocate the buildings and space them out a bit, check their rotations, then retry.
  • Build two Constructors (Iron Plate) and two Smelters in front of the 2nd Iron node as shown.
  • Build one Constructor (Wire) and one Smelter in front of the nearest Copper node as shown.
  • Build one more Constructor (Concrete) in front of the Limestone node, beside the existing Concrete Constructor.
  • Link Smelter and Constructor with Conveyor Belts. Provide Power to all of these buildings.
  • To remove the To-Do List, open Build menu, click 'Edit To-Do List' at the right, then click 'Clear List'.
  • Set the machines' recipe as shown below.
  • Feed the Smelters, Constructors and Burners with ingredients.

  • A Power Pole can be inserted at the middle of an existing Power Line
  • While dismantling, hold down the mass dismantle key and mouse over the target(s) to highlight them all
  • Take note of the machine input and output directions when building

Tier 1 and Tier 2

  • Remove all the Leaves from both Biomass Burners. Just let the power to trip.
  • Craft all Leaves and some Woods into Biomass until you have 400 of them.
    • Do not craft too much Biomass from Woods as Biomass take up more item slots than Woods.
  • Feed both Burners with Biomass and restore the power. Observe Biomass burns much longer than Leaves (and Woods).
  • As the construction of Biomass Burners has unlocked, build 14 Biomass Burners, away from other buildings. Link them to the existing power grid.
    • Make use of the To Do-List.
    • Optional: Build Biomass Burners progressively while expanding the factories.
  • In the inventory, excess Ores can be deleted dragging them into the Trash Can.
  • Convert all the remaining Woods into Biomass.
  • Try stack splitting function:
    • Right-click on a full stack of Biomass to split it into halves (100 each).
    • Or, hold Right-click on the item stack for custom splitting, using slider or textbox.
  • Try feed 100 Biomass into the Biomass Burner using shift-click ⇧ Shift + .
    • Control-click Ctrl + will feed a full stack instead, rendering the stack splitting useless.
    • Feed all Burners. We will unlock a method to gather Biomass quicker in the upcoming part.
  • Craft Leaves and Woods into Biomass to burn them more efficiently
  • Feed Biomass to Burner then reset the breaker
  • Build 14 more Biomass Burners at the edge of the base
  • Right-click an item stack to split it in halves. Hold Right-click for custom split
  • Observe the increase in power capacity.

Logistics - Splitters and Mergers

  • Unlock Milestone - Logistics. Conveyor Splitter and Conveyor Merger are now unlocked.
  • Pick up the Portable Miners on the Iron node.
  • Build a Miner Mk.1 there, facing the Smelter. Take note only 1 Miner can only be built on each node. The miner always has to sit on the center of the node, but is free to rotate.
  • Build a belt connecting the Miner and the nearest Smelter. Connect the Miner with Power.
  • Build a Storage Container at the end of Constructor to receive the product and to keep the Constructor working.
    • Make sure the Storage Container is rotated correctly. Observe the arrow directions before building.
    • Open E Storage Container to take out the products.
    • Optional: Build Container last, after all the steps below are completed, as to save up the Iron Plates and Rods in the meantime.
  • Go to the next Iron node and build another Miner, facing one of the two Smelters. Connect the belt.
  • Build a Splitter at the middle of the belt between the Miner and Smelter, similar to inserting Power Pole at the middle of Power Line.
    • Make sure the Splitter is snapped to the belt instead of sitting on the ground.
    • Connect the side output of the Splitter to the other Smelter as shown.
    • It is fine to build the Splitter if its hologram shows Blue or Yellow color. It is nor possible to build if the Splitter shows red color.
  • At the Constructor output side, build a Storage Container and belt it to a Constructor. Use Merger to combine two belts into one.
  • Tips: The Pioneer can slide below conveyor belt to cross it. To jump onto a Splitter, jump then crouch mid-air while moving forward.
  • Do the same for the Concrete Constructor and Wire Constructor.
  • Improve the production of Wires and Cables as shown below.
  • While using the 'Snap To Guidelines' function, some buildings may be raised off the ground. This is due to plants or stones blocking it.
    • Workaround by using manual placement.
  • Use any free Iron node and make Screw production line as shown.
    • The math / building ratio for Screws is a bit off here, we will improve this at later stage.
  • Expand the Iron Rod production line as shown.
  • While waiting the materials to build up, take time to study the math behind each of the production line above by looking at the machine stats (open their UI).
  • Only one Miner can be built on a node. Rotate it as to face the nearest Smelter
  • An example of fully automated production line, consist of a Miner, a Smelter, a Constructor and a Container, with Belts connected.
  • A Splitter can be built onto an existing belt. Same applies to Merger
  • Two lines of machines producing Iron Plates
  • Use manual alignment to bypass the encroachment hitbox of foliage
  • Wire and Cable production from a single Copper node
  • Make 2 lines for Screw production for a total of 80/min
  • Improve the Iron Rod production from earlier steps, by branching out the items

Obstacle Clearing - Chainsaw and Solid Biofuel

  • Unlock Tier 2 - Obstacle Clearing.
  • Craft a Chainsaw at Equipment Workshop .
  • Create 2 Lines of Solid Biofuel production lines near the Burners:
    • Line 1: Container (Leaves) -> Constructor (Biomass - Leaves) -> Constructor (Solid Biofuel) -> Container
    • Line 2: Container (Woods) -> Constructor (Biomass - Wood) -> Constructor (Solid Biofuel) -> Container
  • Solid Biofuel must be present in Pioneer's inventory for a Chainsaw to work. Look at the lower left to check how much Solid Biofuel is remaining.
  • Collect more biomass by cutting down trees and corals around the base. Observe the area of effect of the Chainsaw.
    • Rock and stone near the Chainsaw AOE will also be removed, giving Limestone. Limestone can be deleted by dragging them into trash can.
    • Keep Silica , Mycelia and Flower Petals for later use.
    • Avoid cutting the Nut tree and Paleberry flower, as their fruits regrow every three days (150 minutes in real-time).
  • If run out of Solid Biofuel, make a Craft Bench on the spot and craft some Solid Biofuel from the Leaves and Woods gathered.
  • Put Leaves and Woods into Containers, and let Constructors to craft them into Solid Biofuel.
    • Machines will not accept wrong type of input items, so items may sometimes be seen stuck on the conveyor belt. In that case, remove all the incorrect items from the upstream (eg, Container) and re-build the feeding belt.
    • Items can be picked up from belt E .
  • Optional: Remove Biomass from Burners and turn them into Solid Biofuel. Feed Solid Biofuel into Burners for better fuel efficiency.
    • To do that, build another line: Container (Biomass) -> Constructor (Solid Biofuel) -> Container
  • Make 2 lines of Solid Biofuel production. One from Leaves, one from Woods.
  • Use Chainsaw to cut down trees and corals. Avoid cutting Nut trees.

Part Assembly

  • Unlock Tier 2 - Part Assembly.
  • Build 2 Assembler near The HUB and set each of them to craft Reinforced Iron Plate and Rotor .
  • On a free Iron node, build 4 additional Constructors crafting Screws as shown.
  • Continue feeding the Assemblers to get a continuous supply of Reinforced Iron Plates and Rotors.
  • These 2 items will be fully automated after Foundation is unlocked, the current hand-feeding stage is only for temporary.
  • Build another copy of Concrete setup at the downhill Limestone node, southwest of the nearest Limestone node.
    • Build the output container uphill, near the existing Concrete container so you don't have to go downhill every time to collect items.

Resource Sink Bonus Program

  • Unlock Tier 2 - Resource Sink Bonus Program
  • Near The HUB, build an AWESOME Shop . Open it E and browse through its pages.
  • Near the AWESOME Shop, build an AWESOME Sink connected with a Storage Container with a belt . Power the Sink via Power Line.
  • Sink excess materials (by feeding items via connected Storage Container) to gain sink points.
    • Craft for a total of 40 Xeno-Zapper and sink them. Generally complex items provide more points.
  • Items fed into the Sink will get destroyed, while giving points which can be used to print FICSIT Coupon s.
  • Open the Sink and print Tickets. Then Ctrl-click Ctrl + the printed ticket into inventory.
  • Open Shop. Navigate to 'Architecture' tab, scroll down, click 'Factory Ladder'. Click 'Add to Cart'. Open the Cart then click 'Buy All'.
  • Ladder is now unlocked. Try building it on the ground and test it.
    • When building a ladder, first click the ground, the mouse up to extend its height. Click again to confirm build.
  • Try the following:
    • Climb up to the top of ladder
    • Build another ladder on top of the ladder
    • Jump off the ladder, then hog to the ladder mid fall. If done correctly the Pioneer should receive no fall damage. Pioneer must be facing the ladder during the fall.
    • Dismantle the ladder after the test. We will build it somewhere else.
  • Buy the following at the Shop:
    • Management -> Conveyor Lift Floor Hole
    • Foundations -> Double Ramp Set
    • Architecture -> Modern Railing
    • FICSIT Specials -> The Boombox and Goat Music Boombox Tape
    • Optional: Architecture -> Modern Catwalks
  • When there is no more item to sink, disconnect the Power of AWESOME Sink to save power.

Base Building - Foundations

  • Unlock Tier 1 - Base Building
  • Near Iron node with factory buildings, build a Foundation 8m x 4m on the ground while holding 'Snap To Guidelines' dynamic key Ctrl . This will snap the first Foundation to the world grid.

  • Bring several stacks of Concrete and Iron Plate, expand the foundation to roughly 20 x 10 tiles.
