The Quantum Encoder is a building used for producing late-game items. Every recipe in the Quantum Encoder consumes Excited Photonic Matter and produces Dark Matter Residue in 1:1 ratios.

It has a fluctuating power demand, considerably more than Particle Accelerators .

Overclocking

Main article: Clock speed

The Quantum Encoder can be overclocked using Power Shards . Overclocking increases the input/output speed of the Quantum Encoder at the cost of exponentially increased power demand.

Power fluctuation

Unlike the Particle Accelerator and Converter whose power consumption ramps up or down linearly, on the Quantum Encoder's power draw follows erratic pattern during the production cycle, instantly changing every 10% of each cycle. The average power consumption is 1000 MW, and the maximum is 2000 MW.

History

  • Patch 1.0
    • Reintroduced with a different model which retains some of the inspiration about the old mesh like the arms.
    • The most high-tech production building in the game. The Quantum Encoder is used to produce the most complex parts, down to the quantum level, such as Superposition Oscillators and Neural-Quantum Processors.
    • The Quantum Encoder requires Excited Photonic Matter to operate and produces Dark Matter Residue as a byproduct.
  • Patch 0.1.5 : Removed. Before its removal, its cost was 20 Heavy Modular Frames, 10 Crystal Oscillators, 10 Supercomputers and 10 High-Speed Connectors. It had 2 Conveyor inputs, 1 Conveyor output and a power usage of 40 MW.
  • Unknown Closed Alpha patch: Introduced, but unobtainable without save editing.
