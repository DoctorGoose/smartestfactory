The **Nobelisk Detonator** is a piece of handheld equipment used to detonate **Nobelisks**. They create explosions, which can be used to remove [cracked boulders](/wiki/Cracked_boulder "Cracked boulder"), [Spore Flowers](/wiki/Spore_Flower "Spore Flower"), [Gas Pillars](/wiki/Gas_Pillar "Gas Pillar"), [Flora](/wiki/Flora "Flora") and [Fauna](/wiki/Fauna "Fauna").

## Usage

### Equipment

As with other equipment, Nobelisk Detonators have to be equipped to be used. Nobelisks serve as 'ammo' for the Detonator and have to be reloaded with [`R`](/wiki/Controls "Controls") for arming. Holding [`R`](/wiki/Controls "Controls") allows to select the ammo type. 

Nobelisks can be thrown with [](/wiki/File:Keyboard_White_Mouse_Left.png "Left"), and holding down allows throwing farther. Running forward while throwing will increase the throw range. Multiple Nobelisks can be deployed before exploding them, by reloading instead of detonating. Nobelisks will stick to any surface they touch when thrown, including creatures and other Nobelisks. A thrown Nobelisk cannot be retrieved in any way. 

After at least one Nobelisk has been thrown, [](/wiki/File:Keyboard_White_Mouse_Right.png "Right") is used to detonate. Multiple thrown Nobelisks will be detonated to create chains of explosions. Alternatively, striking a Nobelisk with any weapon (or having it hit by a [Spitter](/wiki/Spitter "Spitter") spit) will detonate only that one. 

### Nobelisk behavior

When detonated, each Nobelisk blows up in a 7.5-meter radius, dealing up to 50 damage at the center. Damage quickly falls off farther from the center of the explosion, making it almost impossible to deal the full 50 points of damage due to the collision box between it and the target. Therefore, at least 3 Nobelisk explosions are required to kill a fully-healed [pioneer](/wiki/Pioneer "Pioneer"). 

Sticking a Nobelisk on a valid target will cause minor damage of 1 (1/100 of [pioneer](/wiki/Pioneer "Pioneer") HP). If the creature or object the Nobelisk is stuck to dies or is destroyed, the Nobelisk will disappear. Sticking a Nobelisk to a building then dismantling the building will cause the it to stay in mid-air. Nobelisks will stick to and follow moving vehicles, including [the HUB](/wiki/The_HUB "The HUB")'s [FICSIT Freighter](/wiki/FICSIT_Freighter "FICSIT Freighter"). 

[Cracked boulders](/wiki/Cracked_boulder "Cracked boulder"), [Spore Flowers](/wiki/Spore_Flower "Spore Flower") and [Gas Pillars](/wiki/Gas_Pillar "Gas Pillar") can only be destroyed using either Nobelisks or [Explosive Rebar](/wiki/Explosive_Rebar "Explosive Rebar"). Nobelisks do not damage player structures, vehicles, or alter the terrain (other than foliage and select small rocks) - despite implying it could potentially destroy these things, the line "Ensure all FICSIT property is clear of the blast zone before detonation" in the Nuke Nobelisk's description refers to the pioneer only. 

  


Cluster Nobelisk explodes at the center, then creates 5 weaker explosions in a star shape. 

Nuke Nobelisk can one-shot Pioneers in a massive explosion radius. It also deals lingering radiation damage, which can be outpaced by the Pioneer's emergency health regen. 

Gas Nobelisks deals a miniscule 5 damage per second but lasts for a long duration. 

Pulse Nobelisks create a powerful shockwave that pushes creatures, vehicles, and pioneers but does not deal much damage. 

The total number of chained Nobelisk explosions allowed is 255 _(equal to hexadecimal FF)_ , which means using more then 255 in one operation will require multiple uses of the Nobelisk Detonator.[1]

### Explosion cover

The damage of a [Nobelisk](/wiki/Nobelisk "Nobelisk") can be blocked by any physical cover such as [Walls](/wiki/Wall "Wall"), [Conveyor Lift](/wiki/Conveyor_Lift "Conveyor Lift") and [Tree](/wiki/Tree "Tree"). The knock-back effect, however, can still pass through.

## Tips

  * Standing on high ground and aiming at 30 to 45 degrees upward can result in a very long throw.
  * Be careful around [Spitters](/wiki/Spitter "Spitter") and [Alpha Spitters](/wiki/Alpha_Spitter "Alpha Spitter") as their spits will detonate thrown Nobelisks, as mentioned above.
  * When descending midair, it can be very hard to aim the throw as the pioneer's velocity is added to the Nobelisk.
  * When multiple Nobelisks are placed they will explode in a rapid series instead of all at once, which allows you to create a very rudimentary form of propulsion by attaching a large number of Nobelisks to the back of a vehicle. It is possible for them to attach directly to a pioneer, but this will likely kill them mid-flight so it is best to attach them to a vehicle. When using this method of transportation make sure that all of your Nobelisks are connected to the vehicle itself and not other Nobelisks, or else it will cut the "engine" and stop working.
  * Another--and arguably more amusing--method of Nobelisk transportation is to place a large number of them at your feet and hit them with a Xeno-Zapper; this will detonate all the Nobelisks at once and cause the pioneer to rocket into the sky. 
    * This can be used with Pulse Nobelisks for long distance travel, especially in conjunction with [Jetpacks](/wiki/Jetpack "Jetpack") and [Parachutes](/wiki/Parachute "Parachute"). Approximately 7-9 will get the player to any point on the map with some practice.
  * If you carry more than one detonator, they will only trigger the Nobelisks deployed with the given detonator (assuming they are deployed away sufficiently far from each other).



## Trivia

  * Nobelisks thrown to the face of a charging [Hog](/wiki/Hog "Hog") or [Alpha Hog](/wiki/Alpha_Hog "Alpha Hog") will stagger them and stop the charge, just like the [Xeno-Zapper](/wiki/Xeno-Zapper "Xeno-Zapper") or [Xeno-Basher](/wiki/Xeno-Basher "Xeno-Basher") do. This will also scare off the [Land Whale](/wiki/Space_Giraffe-Tick-Penguin-Whale_Thing "Space Giraffe-Tick-Penguin-Whale Thing").
  * It seems that aliens receive more damage from an explosion the closer it is to the center of their body. The [Land Whale](/wiki/Space_Giraffe-Tick-Penguin-Whale_Thing "Space Giraffe-Tick-Penguin-Whale Thing") takes ~20 damage if a regular Nobelisk is attached to its leg, ~27 to its head or tail, and ~34 damage to its chest. 
    * Despite this, [pioneers](/wiki/Pioneer "Pioneer") will take more damage from an explosion the closer the _feet_ are to the Nobelisk. Standing right on top of one at the time of the explosion deals 47 damage.
  * A single, well placed Nobelisk can one-shot a [Hog](/wiki/Hog "Hog") or a [Spitter](/wiki/Spitter "Spitter").
  * The name 'Nobelisk' may refer to [Alfred Nobel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfred_Nobel "wikipedia:Alfred Nobel"), the inventor of [Dynamite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamite "wikipedia:Dynamite").
  * The Nobelisk Detonator is the smallest item in-game.

## Current Issue

Unexploded Nobelisks will still appear upon reload of Game Save and will automatically explode shortly after starting gameplay. After placing one (or more) Nobelisks and using the Nobelisk Detonator, at the location where the unexploded Nobelisks _were_ upon Game Save load, you will see an "explosion".[2][3]
