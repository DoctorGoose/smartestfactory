The **Cliff Hog** and **Nuclear Hog** are hostile creatures that can be encountered while exploring. They have a melee charging attack, as well as a rock-throwing ranged attack. 

The Nuclear Hog is a stronger variant of the Cliff Hog. It is distinguished by a green glow over its body, and has more health and emits a passive [radiation](/wiki/Radiation "Radiation") effect. The radiation intensity fills the radiation bar completely when standing up close.

## Behavior

Both the Cliff and Nuclear Hog can be mostly found patrolling [Crash Sites](/wiki/Crash_Site "Crash Site"), [Power Slugs](/wiki/Power_Slug "Power Slug") and [artifacts](/wiki/Artifacts "Artifacts"), as well as entrances to late-game areas of the game, such as the [Red Bamboo Fields](/wiki/Red_Bamboo_Fields "Red Bamboo Fields"). The Cliff Hog can typically be found alone or in groups of two, whereas the Nuclear Hog can typically be found alone. 

### Cliff Hog

  * Higher health, damage, size, and speed relative to the [Alpha Hog](/wiki/Alpha_Hog "Alpha Hog")
  * Basic Hog Charge attack, with increased speed and damage
  * New Rock Throw attack, which launches a rock at a distant or unreachable target
  * New Plow Charge attack, which continually pushes and deals damage to the target



### Nuclear Hog

  * Higher health, damage, size, and speed relative to the Cliff Hog
  * Basic Hog Charge attack, with increased speed and damage
  * New Rock Throw attack, which launches a rock at a distant or unreachable target
  * New Plow Charge attack, which continually pushes and deals damage to the target
  * Passive [Radiation](/wiki/Radiation "Radiation") Damage Area of Effect (AoE)



### Spawning

Both the Cliff and Nuclear Hog spawn singularly, and can be prevented from spawning by using [creature respawn prevention methods](/wiki/Creatures#Respawning "Creatures"). 

## Combat

A viable strategy is stunning the hog using a [](/wiki/Rebar_Gun "Rebar Gun") [Rebar Gun](/wiki/Rebar_Gun "Rebar Gun") with [](/wiki/Stun_Rebar "Stun Rebar") [Stun Rebar](/wiki/Stun_Rebar "Stun Rebar"), then attacking it from the side using a [](/wiki/Xeno-Basher "Xeno-Basher") [Xeno-Basher](/wiki/Xeno-Basher "Xeno-Basher"). This prevents it from attacking immediately after the stun wears off. 

The [](/wiki/Rifle "Rifle") [Rifle](/wiki/Rifle "Rifle") will not kill even the weaker Cliff Hog in one magazine if not using [](/wiki/Turbo_Rifle_Ammo "Turbo Rifle Ammo") [Turbo Rifle Ammo](/wiki/Turbo_Rifle_Ammo "Turbo Rifle Ammo"), but it does leave it at fairly low HP; emptying a Rifle magazine into a Cliff Hog followed by one [](/wiki/Explosive_Rebar "Explosive Rebar") [Explosive Rebar](/wiki/Explosive_Rebar "Explosive Rebar") or (provided enough pellets hit)[](/wiki/Shatter_Rebar "Shatter Rebar") [Shatter Rebar](/wiki/Shatter_Rebar "Shatter Rebar") shot is enough to kill it. 

Using [](/wiki/Nobelisk "Nobelisk") [Nobelisks](/wiki/Nobelisk "Nobelisk") is also too slow, with the exception of [](/wiki/Nuke_Nobelisk "Nuke Nobelisk") [Nuke Nobelisks](/wiki/Nuke_Nobelisk "Nuke Nobelisk"), which may however hurt you while detonating. 

If the hog is near a tall cliff, throwing it off using a [](/wiki/Pulse_Nobelisk "Pulse Nobelisk") [Pulse Nobelisks](/wiki/Pulse_Nobelisk "Pulse Nobelisk") might allow avoiding the fight entirely, although this is a bit risky and requires the hog to be in the right location. Speaking of location, consider accelerating a vehicle into them as many times as you can afford fuel since the vehicle protects you both from radiation and regular fall damage should you require those functions. Takes some ragdoll animations until they're defeated. 

If flying over the charge, [](/wiki/Packaged_Turbofuel "Packaged Turbofuel") [Turbofuel](/wiki/Packaged_Turbofuel "Packaged Turbofuel") or better is required for the [](/wiki/Jetpack "Jetpack") [Jetpack](/wiki/Jetpack "Jetpack"). Wearing a [](/wiki/Hazmat_Suit "Hazmat Suit") [Hazmat Suit](/wiki/Hazmat_Suit "Hazmat Suit") nullifies the radiation damage from Nuclear Hogs while filters last. 

### Death

Upon death, the Nuclear Hog corpse will remain radioactive until it despawns. The [](/wiki/Hog_Remains "Hog Remains") [Hog Remains](/wiki/Hog_Remains "Hog Remains") for the Nuclear Hog is not radioactive and can be collected without any concerns.
