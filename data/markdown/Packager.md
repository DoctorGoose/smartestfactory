The **Packager** is a building used to package and unpackage [fluids](/wiki/Fluids "Fluids") in and out of [](/wiki/Empty_Canister "Empty Canister") [Empty Canisters](/wiki/Empty_Canister "Empty Canister") or [](/wiki/Empty_Fluid_Tank "Empty Fluid Tank") [Empty Fluid Tanks](/wiki/Empty_Fluid_Tank "Empty Fluid Tank"). Both Canisters and Fluid Tanks are returned when fluid is unpackaged from them, allowing them to be reused. Packaged liquids can be transported with [Conveyor Belts](/wiki/Conveyor_Belt "Conveyor Belt") \- this can be more efficient when transporting them with [Conveyor Lifts](/wiki/Conveyor_Lift "Conveyor Lift") due to the power usage of [Pipeline Pumps](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump"), but only for very large vertical distances.

## Overclocking

_Main article:_[Clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed")

The Packager can be overclocked using [Power Shards](/wiki/Power_Shard "Power Shard"). Overclocking increases the input/output speed of the Packager at the cost of exponentially increased [power](/wiki/Power "Power") demand. 

Clock speed | 25% | 50% | 75% | 100% | 150% | 200% | 250%   
---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---  
Consumption (MW)  | 1.6 | 4 | 6.84 | 10 | 17.09 | 25 | 33.58   
  
## Head lift

_Main article:_[Head lift](/wiki/Head_lift "Head lift")

The Packager has a recommended [head lift](/wiki/Head_lift "Head lift") of 10 meters, measured from the center of its pipe outlet. 

## Unpackageable Fluids

The following fluids are not able to be packaged: 

  * [Dissolved Silica](/wiki/Dissolved_Silica "Dissolved Silica")
  * [Excited Photonic Matter](/wiki/Excited_Photonic_Matter "Excited Photonic Matter")
  * [Dark Matter Residue](/wiki/Dark_Matter_Residue "Dark Matter Residue")



## Using for vertical transport of fluids

For vertical transport of fluids, using Packagers and [Conveyor Lifts](/wiki/Conveyor_Lift "Conveyor Lift") instead of [Pipelines](/wiki/Pipeline "Pipeline") and [Pipeline Pumps](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") can be more efficient, but only for extremely large vertical distances, due to the number of Packagers required to package/unpackage a full [Pipeline](/wiki/Pipeline "Pipeline") of fluid. The break-even point varies by fluid as they all have different package/unpackage rates. 

To approximately calculate the break-even amount of vertcal lift you: 

  * Calculate the number of Packagers required to package and unpackage a full [Pipeline](/wiki/Pipeline "Pipeline") (300/s for mk1 or 600/s for mk2) of fluid 
    * [Pipeline](/wiki/Pipeline "Pipeline") flow rate / package rate of fluid
    * Plus [Pipeline](/wiki/Pipeline "Pipeline") flow rate / unpackage rate of fluid
  * Calculate the energy these Packagers require 
    * Packager count * 10MW
    * Note this is simplified as it does not account for the lower-than-proportional energy usage of an [underclocked](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed") machine, or the higher-than-proportional energy usage of a partially idle machine
  * Calculate the number of [Pipeline Pumps](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") that can be powered for the same energy 
    * Packager energy / [Pipeline Pump](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") power
    * (4MW for a mk1, 8MW for a mk2)
    * Round the [Pipeline Pump](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") count down to the nearest whole number (as they do not [underclock](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed"))
  * Calculate the total [Head lift](/wiki/Head_lift "Head lift") this many [Pipeline Pumps](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") will provide 
    * [Pipeline Pump](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") count * [Pipeline Pump](/wiki/Pipeline_Pump "Pipeline Pump") [Head lift](/wiki/Head_lift "Head lift")
    * (20m for a mk1, 50m for a mk2)
  * This is the approximate break-even point



The equation for this is: 
    
    
    FLOOR( ( ( (pipe flow rate / package rate) + (pipe flow rate / unpackage rate) ) * packager power) / pump power ) * pump head lift
    

  
Where FLOOR() is a function that rounds down to the nearest integer. 

Note this equation does not account for [underclocked](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed") or partially idle Packagers, where a fractional number of Packagers is needed (e.g. 2.5 Packagers are needed to unpackage 300/s [Water](/wiki/Water "Water")). 

  * If fractional Packagers are [underclocked](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed") to the exact required fraction then power use and break-even will be slightly less than this equation indicates due to better power efficiency
  * If fractional Packagers are left at 100% [Clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed") and so are partially idle then power use and break-even will be slightly more than this equation indicates due to idle power draw

## Trivia

  * "PAC-IT MK1" is written on the machine, suggesting that higher marks would be available, though it has been denied by developers.[1]
  * Before the introduction of the Packager, [Refineries](/wiki/Refinery "Refinery") were given the job of packaging and unpackaging liquids. [Refineries](/wiki/Refinery "Refinery") that were constructed after the update lost this ability.
  * To calculate the [energy](/wiki/Energy "Energy") spent to package / unpackage a certain fluid: 
    * ` Energy = 600 MJ / (rate of un/packaging in X/min) `. The multiplier for the item recipe can be ignored as only the rate is concerned.
  * Occasionally the packager does an animation accompanied with audio that looks like its overloading and throwing the fluid around. This is only graphical, production is not affected.
  * Packagers are the only production [building](/wiki/Buildings "Buildings") that cannot be [amplified](/wiki/Production_amplifier "Production amplifier") with [](/wiki/Somersloop "Somersloop") [Somersloops](/wiki/Somersloop "Somersloop"), as this would allow for any packageable fluid to be produced anywhere in the world (and for the creation of infinite [](/wiki/Empty_Canister "Empty Canister") [Empty Canisters](/wiki/Empty_Canister "Empty Canister") / [](/wiki/Empty_Fluid_Tank "Empty Fluid Tank") [Empty Fluid Tanks](/wiki/Empty_Fluid_Tank "Empty Fluid Tank")).
