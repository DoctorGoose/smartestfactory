# Foundations

**Foundations** are tile-able structures that can be used to make platforms for other [buildings](/wiki/Buildings "Buildings") to be built on. When stacked on each other with adequate spacing, they can be used to build multi-story factories. Foundations provide a grid of snap points for all buildings and allow for the construction of [walls](/wiki/Walls "Walls") and [walkways](/wiki/Walkways "Walkways"), facilitating the construction of organized factories.

## Usage

### Construction

Foundations primarily serve as factory floors. Placing structures on top of them aligns the structures to a grid with one meter steps. 

Foundations can be placed one at a time or up to 10 at the same in time with the [Zoop](/wiki/Build_Gun#Zoop "Build Gun") build mode. 

The first Foundation has to be placed on the ground, while additional Foundations attach to it, allowing them to be placed higher. Due to the absence of building physics, dismantling any supports will cause them to float. Building Foundations too close to the ground or on sloped terrain may cause the terrain or grass to clip through, which can only be solved by building the Foundation higher up. By holding [`Ctrl`](/wiki/Controls "Controls") while not pointing at other foundations, foundations are snapped to the world grid. The world grid is snapped to the nearest 800 cm in x-z direction and nearest 100 cm in y-direction, which can be seen in a third-party save editor. 

Multiple floors can be constructed using Foundations. [Walls](/wiki/Walls "Walls") can only be built attached to a Foundation or another Wall. 

Ramps can be built standalone, but work well by building them next to Foundations, allowing [Pioneers](/wiki/Pioneer "Pioneer") and [Vehicles](/wiki/Vehicles "Vehicles") to get on Foundation floors or over rough terrain. When [Zooping](/wiki/Build_Gun#Zoop "Build Gun") Ramps, they will automatically snap diagonally and there is no need to build a flat foundation prior to building another Ramp. [1] Inverted Ramps and Quarter Pipes are purely decorative. 

#### Rotation

  * Foundations can be rotated before placement in 45° increments.
  * Additionally while a Foundation is on top of another Foundation you can hold [`Ctrl`](/wiki/Controls "Controls") Key and rotate in fine increments of 5°, or 9 steps per 45° of rotation. [2]
  * Foundation fine adjustment along with [Vertical Zooping](/wiki/Build_Gun#Zoop "Build Gun") will allow building spiral columns, circles, or other great designs.


  * [](/wiki/File:Foundation_Rotation.png "Update 5 - Foundation Fine Rotation Example \(right side rotated 90° in 18 steps\)")

Update 5 - Foundation Fine Rotation Example _(right side rotated 90° in 18 steps)_




### Exploration

Foundation bridges or towers can be built fairly easily, requiring nothing other than [](/wiki/Concrete "Concrete") [Concrete](/wiki/Concrete "Concrete"). This allows crossing infinitely large gaps or any climps with the theoretical minimum of 6 Concrete, enough to build 2 Inverted 1m Down Corners, by building one and carefully dismantling the other. 

Double Ramps can be built rapidly, allowing vertical bridges to be built with ease. 

### World grid

Holding [`Ctrl`](/wiki/Controls "Controls") when placing a foundation oriented to a cardinal direction (north) will align it to the world grid. There is only one world grid based on global coordinates. It is not possible to shift the origin of the world grid or use it in other rotations. 

[Resource Nodes](/wiki/Resource_Node "Resource Node") and [Resource Wells](/wiki/Resource_Well "Resource Well") aren't aligned to the world grid, thus [Miners](/wiki/Miner "Miner") and other extractors placed on them cannot be aligned to it, rather to the center of each node/well. 

Before [Patch 1.0](/wiki/Patch_1.0 "Patch 1.0"), 1m foundations would snap to the world grid with a 0.5m offset. 

## Tips

  * It is a good idea to always have enough [Concrete](/wiki/Concrete "Concrete") and [Iron Plates](/wiki/Iron_Plate "Iron Plate") in your inventory when you go exploring so that you can create bridges and ramps with Foundations to reach places you cannot otherwise walk, jump, or fly to.
  * In a pinch, it is easy to make extra [Concrete](/wiki/Concrete "Concrete") in the [Craft Bench](/wiki/Craft_Bench "Craft Bench") manually mined from [Limestone](/wiki/Limestone "Limestone") chunks which you can find all over the map.
  * Foundations can be built on natural cliffs with a positive slope.
  * Foundations can be built on top of items dropped by a pioneer, such as [Wire](/wiki/Wire "Wire").
  * Quarter Pipes can be combined to form a tunnel-like structure or even a tunnel network, to form pioneer-made 'underground' networks.
  * When built in a spiral, Stairs have a square hole in the center of the stack that leaves enough clearance to fit a vertical [Jump Pad](/wiki/Jump_Pad "Jump Pad"), or a pillar.
  * Corner Ramps up do not have an encroachment box, so they are ideal for covering up holes between foundations due to intersecting infrastructure such as Conveyor Lifts and Pipes.
