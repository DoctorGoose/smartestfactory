# Old unreleased content

This page lists **old unreleased content** which was datamined from old builds of the game and will not be implemented into the game, according to an [official video by Coffee Stain](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-JXPsTlJIiI&t=83). 

This page is being preserved for archival purposes. As a matter of policy no _new_ unreleased content should be added here, but additional information regarding the existing content is still considered relevant.

## Heat Resistant Suit

## Heat Resistant Suit

[ ](/wiki/File:Heat_Resistant_Suit.png "Heat Resistant Suit.png")

_Used to avoid heat damage._

The **Heat Resistant Suit** would have been used to prevent the engineer from taking any heat damage. It is unknown where it would get used, as currently there is no heat damage (unlike [Radiation](/wiki/Radiation "Radiation") and its [suit](/wiki/Hazmat_Suit "Hazmat Suit") for example). It didn't change the engineer's appearance.

The **Rebar Scatter Gun** was a ranged weapon that could be unlocked in [Patch 0.1.5](/wiki/Patch_0.1.5 "Patch 0.1.5") and was removed in [Patch 0.1.6](/wiki/Patch_0.1.6 "Patch 0.1.6"). It couldn't be reloaded with [](/wiki/Spiked_Rebar "Spiked Rebar") [Spiked Rebar](/wiki/Spiked_Rebar "Spiked Rebar"), rendering it unusable. It did have it's own ammo, but it could not be crafted. The only way to get it was save editing and/or cheats. 

Its functionality has been reintroduced in [Patch 0.6.0.0](/wiki/Patch_0.6.0.0 "Patch 0.6.0.0") with the [](/wiki/Shatter_Rebar "Shatter Rebar") [Shatter Rebar](/wiki/Shatter_Rebar "Shatter Rebar") alternative ammo for the [](/wiki/Rebar_Gun "Rebar Gun") [Rebar Gun](/wiki/Rebar_Gun "Rebar Gun").

## Mk.2 variants of buildings

The [](/wiki/Assembler "Assembler") [Assembler](/wiki/Assembler "Assembler"), [](/wiki/Constructor "Constructor") [Constructor](/wiki/Constructor "Constructor"), [](/wiki/Foundry "Foundry") [Foundry](/wiki/Foundry "Foundry"), [](/wiki/Manufacturer "Manufacturer") [Manufacturer](/wiki/Manufacturer "Manufacturer") and [](/wiki/Smelter "Smelter") [Smelter](/wiki/Smelter "Smelter") had a Mk.2 variant that would operate either 25% or 50% faster (listed in the infoboxes below). 

These buildings weren't introduced because Coffee Stain felt they would need unique designs, which takes a lot of work just for them to be the same but faster, especially with [overclocking](/wiki/Overclocking "Overclocking") available.

The **Converter** was a building that could convert any two raw resources into any one raw resource of the player's choice. It used a temporary cube model with a default UE4 Material and did function. 

Even though it was intended to convert two resources into a new one, it only needed one to be functional. 

It was eventually implemented in [Patch 1.0](/wiki/Patch_1.0 "Patch 1.0") with additional uses and designated recipes to make new ores.

The **Hadron Collider** was a building used to produce [Dark Matter](/wiki/Dark_Matter "Dark Matter") and having one output and zero inputs. Due to not being implemented correctly, the recipe could not be selected and the building was never operational. 

The Hadron Collider was eventually implemented in [Patch 0.4.0.0](/wiki/Patch_0.4.0.0 "Patch 0.4.0.0"), renamed as the [](/wiki/Particle_Accelerator "Particle Accelerator") [Particle Accelerator](/wiki/Particle_Accelerator "Particle Accelerator"), with different functionality. 

It had unusually high power usage, which didn't fluctuate. Additionally, it reportedly wasn't animated, simply lighting up when placed.

## Powered Wall

## Powered Wall

[ ](/wiki/File:Powered_Wall.png "Powered Wall.png")

_When connected to a Power Grid it makes the Floor it stands on and any Floors connected to it propagate power. No More Power Poles or Power Lines on floors!_

## Ingredients

**2×** [](/wiki/Heavy_Modular_Frame "Heavy Modular Frame") [Heavy Modular Frame](/wiki/Heavy_Modular_Frame "Heavy Modular Frame")

**1×** [](/wiki/High-Speed_Connector "High-Speed Connector") [High-Speed Connector](/wiki/High-Speed_Connector "High-Speed Connector")

The **Powered Wall** was a building that allowed power to propagate through [foundations](/wiki/Foundations "Foundations") underneath it, removing the need for [Power Lines](/wiki/Power_Line "Power Line"). 

In some cases, if the Powered Wall was placed down, the game would crash. It was not kept due to being unintuitive. The intended experience was introduced using [Wall Outlets](/wiki/Power_Pole "Power Pole").

The **Mk.6 Conveyor Belt** and **Mk.6 Conveyor Lift** would have been the highest conveyor mark. 

The belt could be accessed and datamined, with its description stating a capacity of 900 parts/min. After its removal from public builds, the capacity was increased to 1200 parts/min, as confirmed by translation strings.[1] They also confirm the existence of a matching Mk.6 lift,[2] which never appeared in a public build. 

It can be assumed the increase happened at the same time the capacity of Mk.4 and Mk.5 conveyors was increased in [Patch 0.1.15](/wiki/Patch_0.1.15 "Patch 0.1.15"). 

The Mk.6 belt is showcased in the [Conveyor Belt Speeds teaser](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IeYAFCJ0N3I) alongside all other Conveyor Belt marks and has been subsequently mentioned in several dev streams. 

It was eventually implemented in [Patch 1.0](/wiki/Patch_1.0 "Patch 1.0") with a different build cost.
