# Fuel-Powered Generator

## Fuel-Powered Generator  
  
[ ](/wiki/File:Fuel-Powered_Generator.png "Fuel-Powered Generator.png")

_Consumes Fuel to generate electricity for the power grid.  
Has a Pipeline input port that allows the Fuel supply to be automated.  
Caution: Always generates power at the set clock speed. Shuts down if fuel requirements are not met._

The **Fuel-Powered Generator** is a [power generator](/wiki/Power_generator "Power generator") [building](/wiki/Building "Building") that generates [power](/wiki/Power "Power") by burning liquid fuel ([](/wiki/Fuel "Fuel") [Fuel](/wiki/Fuel "Fuel"), [](/wiki/Liquid_Biofuel "Liquid Biofuel") [Liquid Biofuel](/wiki/Liquid_Biofuel "Liquid Biofuel"), [](/wiki/Turbofuel "Turbofuel") [Turbofuel](/wiki/Turbofuel "Turbofuel")) or gaseous fuel ([](/wiki/Rocket_Fuel "Rocket Fuel") [Rocket Fuel](/wiki/Rocket_Fuel "Rocket Fuel"), or [](/wiki/Ionized_Fuel "Ionized Fuel") [Ionized Fuel](/wiki/Ionized_Fuel "Ionized Fuel")). 

At 100% [clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed"), one Fuel-Powered Generator produces 250 MW. The type of fuel does not affect power production, only burning time.

## Fuel consumption

Fuel | Energy | Burn duration | Burn rate   
---|---|---|---  
[](/wiki/Fuel "Fuel") [Fuel](/wiki/Fuel "Fuel") | 750 MJ | 3 sec | 20 m3/min   
[](/wiki/Liquid_Biofuel "Liquid Biofuel") [Liquid Biofuel](/wiki/Liquid_Biofuel "Liquid Biofuel") | 750 MJ | 3 sec | 20 m3/min[note 1]  
[](/wiki/Turbofuel "Turbofuel") [Turbofuel](/wiki/Turbofuel "Turbofuel") | 2,000 MJ | 8 sec | 7.5 m3/min   
[](/wiki/Rocket_Fuel "Rocket Fuel") [Rocket Fuel](/wiki/Rocket_Fuel "Rocket Fuel") | 3,600 MJ | 14.4 sec | 4.1666... m3/min[note 2]  
[](/wiki/Ionized_Fuel "Ionized Fuel") [Ionized Fuel](/wiki/Ionized_Fuel "Ionized Fuel") | 5,000 MJ | 20 sec | 3 m3/min   
  
Notes: 

  1. ↑ Unlike [Biomass Burners](/wiki/Biomass_Burner "Biomass Burner"), Fuel-Powered Generators don't scale to demand and always burn fuel at the maximum rate. Thus, it has better use being [packaged](/wiki/Packaged_Liquid_Biofuel "Packaged Liquid Biofuel") as [Jetpack](/wiki/Jetpack "Jetpack") fuel.
  2. ↑ This number is derived from the burn duration and is exactly 6014.4 m3/min. Thus, 3 Fuel-Powered generators will consume 12.5 m3/min.



## Overclocking

_Main article:_[Clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed")

The Fuel-Powered Generator can be overclocked using [Power Shards](/wiki/Power_Shard "Power Shard"). Overclocking increases the fuel consumption rate and produced power linearly. The same amount of power is always produced from the same input resources.

## Generators per node

A single [Crude Oil](/wiki/Crude_Oil "Crude Oil") [node](/wiki/Resource_node "Resource node"), using only the original Crude Oil to Fuel recipe, can support the following number of Fuel-Powered Generators operating at peak capacity (fractional amounts of machines mean that the [clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed") of the last machine needs to be adjusted for maximum efficiency):

byproduct/min  | Number of  
Fuel-Powered Generators  | Gross power output*   
---|---|---|---|---|---|---  
**Impure** | 60 m3 | 1  | 40 m3 | 30  | 2  | 500 MW   
**Normal** | 120 m3 | 2  | 80 m3 | 60  | 4  | 1000 MW   
**Pure** | 240 m3 | 4  | 160 m3 | 120  | 8  | 2000 MW   
**Overclock to 150 m 3**** | 150 m3 | 2.5  | 100 m3 | 75  | 5  | 1250 MW   
**Overclock to 300 m 3***** | 300 m3 | 5  | 200 m3 | 150  | 10  | 2500 MW   
**Overclock to 600 m 3****** | 600 m3 | 10  | 400 m3 | 300  | 20  | 5000 MW   
  
  * * Net power capacity is less due to the Oil Extractor and Refineries consuming power
  * ** This is either an impure node at 250% or normal at 125%
  * *** This is either a normal node at 250% or pure at 125%, the limit of Mk.1 Pipelines
  * **** This is a pure node at 250%, the limit of Mk.2 Pipelines



Fuel production can be greatly expanded by making use of [Alt](/wiki/Hard_Drive "Hard Drive") [Diluted (Packaged) Fuel](/wiki/Packaged_Fuel "Packaged Fuel"), even more when combined with [Alt](/wiki/Hard_Drive "Hard Drive") [](/wiki/Heavy_Oil_Residue "Heavy Oil Residue") [Heavy Oil Residue](/wiki/Heavy_Oil_Residue "Heavy Oil Residue").
