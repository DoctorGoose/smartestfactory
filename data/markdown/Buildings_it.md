Un [Assemblatore](/wiki/Assembler/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Assembler/it \(page does not exist\)"), uno dei tanti edifici presenti in _Satisfactory_.

Un **edificio** è qualsiasi struttura posizionata utilizzando lo [Strumento Costruttore](/wiki/Build_Gun/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Build Gun/it \(page does not exist\)"). Ogni edificio richiede materiali per essere costruito, che vengono prelevati dall'[inventario](/wiki/Inventory/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Inventory/it \(page does not exist\)"). Inversamente, ogni edificio può essere [smantellato](/wiki/Dismantle/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Dismantle/it \(page does not exist\)"), restituendo tutti i materiali utilizzati per costruirlo, così come tutte le parti in esso contenute. 

Gli edifici possono essere categorizzati in più gruppi: 

  * Speciali: questi edifici hanno un uso speciale, solitamente legato alla progressione.
  * Produzione: noti anche come "edifici di fabbrica", costituiscono linee di produzione che trasformano parti in altre parti.
  * Energia: comprende sia [Generatori di Energia](/wiki/Power_generator/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Power generator/it \(page does not exist\)") che strutture della [rete elettrica](/wiki/Power_grid/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Power grid/it \(page does not exist\)"), come i [Pali Elettrici](/wiki/Power_Poles/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Power Poles/it \(page does not exist\)"), utilizzati per fornire energia agli edifici che ne hanno bisogno per funzionare.
  * Logistica: costruzioni che collegano edifici produttivi e generatori di energia per trasportare risorse tra di essi, come [Nastri Trasportatori](/wiki/Conveyor_Belts/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Conveyor Belts/it \(page does not exist\)") e [Condutture](/wiki/Pipelines/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Pipelines/it \(page does not exist\)")
  * Trasporto: un'estensione della logistica, trasportando [Pionieri](/wiki/Pioneer/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Pioneer/it \(page does not exist\)") e parti utilizzando [veicoli](/wiki/Vehicles/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Vehicles/it \(page does not exist\)") o altri metodi dedicati.
  * Organizzazione: edifici che servono a diverse utilità, ma che di solito non sono utili da soli.
  * Architecture: [Fondazioni](/wiki/Foundations/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Foundations/it \(page does not exist\)"), [Pareti](/wiki/Walls/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Walls/it \(page does not exist\)") e altre strutture decorative utilizzate per contenere le fabbriche in modo ordinato.

## Ottenimento

Nuovi edifici diventano disponibili in diversi modi. Questi sono [Obiettivi dell'HUB](/wiki/Milestones/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Milestones/it \(page does not exist\)"), [Ricerca nel M.A.M.](/wiki/MAM/?action=edit&redlink=1 "MAM/ \(page does not exist\)"), e gli acquisti nel [Negozio AWESOME](/wiki/AWESOME_Shop/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "AWESOME Shop/it \(page does not exist\)"). Una volta sbloccati, verranno evidenziati nel menu di costruzione fino a quando non verranno passati con il mouse. 

Per dettagli su come vengono costruiti gli edifici, vedi lo [Strumento costruttore](/wiki/Build_Gun/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Build Gun/it \(page does not exist\)"). I [Progetti](/wiki/Blueprint/it?action=edit&redlink=1 "Blueprint/it \(page does not exist\)") possono essere utilizzati per costruire più rapidamente configurazioni pre-costruite. 

## Uso

A typical production line starts at a resource extractor, such as a [Miner](/wiki/Miner "Miner") or [Oil Extractor](/wiki/Oil_Extractor "Oil Extractor"). The extracted resource is then transported using Conveyor Belts, Pipelines or vehicles to a set of production buildings, which process the ore to the result part, usually combining other raw resources. The product is then either sent to other factories, stored in a [Storage Container](/wiki/Storage_Container "Storage Container"), burned off in a power generator or vehicle, or destroyed in an [AWESOME Sink](/wiki/AWESOME_Sink "AWESOME Sink"). 

Most buildings, notably production buildings, can be interacted with using [`E`](/wiki/Controls "Controls"). This allows them to be inspected and configured, such as by selecting which recipe to use, or set the [clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed"). Once configured, they can be inspected to view that they operate as expected. Some buildings can only be inspected (such as [Power Poles](/wiki/Power_Poles "Power Poles")), while others can be configured without bringing up a menu (such as [doors](/wiki/Walls "Walls")), or not at all. 

Production and other buildings require [power](/wiki/Power "Power") to operate, which has to be supplied by connecting them to a power grid using a [Power Line](/wiki/Power_Line "Power Line"). If the consumption of all buildings in the grid exceeds the production of all power generators, a power trip will occur. 

### Velocità di clock

_Main article:_[Clock speed](/wiki/Clock_speed "Clock speed")

Production buildings and power generators can be underclocked or overclocked using [](/wiki/Power_Shard "Power Shard") [Power Shards](/wiki/Power_Shard "Power Shard"). This affects the rate at which they operate and the power they consume (or produce). 

Production buildings' operating rate scales to their clock speed, but their power usage does not. A building at 200% will consume more than twice the power. Resource extractors can only be constructed on the sources of their respective resource. As there is a fixed amount of those on the map, overclocking them increases the total maximum amount of that resource per minute. It also allows resources to be obtained without having to transport them from far away, if an overclocked extractor suffices. For factory buildings, overclocking saves space and simplifies the setup, at the cost of more power. On the other hand, underclocking reduces their power usage. 

Power generators scale directly to their clock speed, with no losses or penalties, other than the cost of Power Shards. 

### Danno

Buildings cannot be damaged in any way, not by pioneer weapons, creatures, or the weather. If enough powered buildings are built in an area, creatures will stop respawning near them. 

### Legs and feet

In Update 3 and earlier most Buildings / Machines always had "Legs" and "Feet". 

In Update 4, the "Legs" and "Feet" on some Buildings / Machines were disabled by default, and are only used now if constructing these Buildings / Machines on an slope / incline. [1]

Some Buildings still have "Legs" / "Feet" such as the [The HUB](/wiki/The_HUB "The HUB"), [Space Elevator](/wiki/Space_Elevator "Space Elevator"), [Nuclear Power Plant](/wiki/Nuclear_Power_Plant "Nuclear Power Plant"), and a few more. 

Constructing on [Foundations](/wiki/Foundations "Foundations") often will eliminate the "Legs" / "Feet", however if certain Buildings / Machines are raised _by moving Mouse upward_ before final placement the Player will see the the "Legs" / "Feet" appear. 

## Nudge mode

• While nudging some buildings with Nudge Mode ([`H`](/wiki/Controls "Controls")+**Arrow Keys**), it is possible to place buildings even if it was in a invalid location (such as the surface being too steep).
