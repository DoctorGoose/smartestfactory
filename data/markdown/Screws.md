## Tips

  * The most resource-efficient way to make Screws from [Iron Ingots](/wiki/Iron_Ingot "Iron Ingot") is converting them to [Steel Ingots](/wiki/Steel_Ingot "Steel Ingot") using the Solid Steel Ingot alternate recipe, processing those to [Iron Rods](/wiki/Iron_Rod "Iron Rod") using the Steel Rod alternate recipe, and finally producing screws using the default recipe, having an efficiency of 24 screws per [Iron Ingot](/wiki/Iron_Ingot "Iron Ingot") or 16 per [Steel Ingot](/wiki/Steel_Ingot "Steel Ingot"). 
    * If the steel is used to make [Steel Beams](/wiki/Steel_Beam "Steel Beam") instead, only 13 Screws per [Steel Ingot](/wiki/Steel_Ingot "Steel Ingot") are made, however, the recipe is far more space and power-efficient.
    * Making 780 Screws per minute from steel requires 23 Constructors using the Steel Rod setup, but only 4 with the Steel Screw setup.
  * As one of the low-compression items, transporting them to the next machine that requires them can be a challenge during the early game, due to the relatively low throughput of the conveyor and high Screw consumption rate. Even late-game their low compression can present problems as the high demand of certain processes can easily overwhelm the capacity of a single Mk. 5 belt: for example, a line with more than 7 Assemblers making Rotors will require two inputs.
  * To avoid having more screws than the belt throughput can handle, feeding directly into the machine that needs them is an option. For example: an assembler making 4 rotors needs 100 screws. Using a single constructor with steel screws at 38.5% capacity can directly feed into the assembler, without first merging.



### Eliminating Screws

By finding the right alternative recipes, it is possible to completely eliminate Screws from normal production lines.

## Trivia

  * The screws shown in the item's mesh do not have threads or tapered ends, so they would not work as screws. Instead, they look more like rivets or machinery pins. 
    * The official explanation is that the box is a big bucket and contains at least one or two screws.[2]
  * Although it appears as a box of screws, it is only counted as one item.
  * 27 Screws are used in [Drop Pods](/wiki/Crash_Site "Crash Site").
